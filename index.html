System Architecture · Data Flow

Five Tiers, One Ecosystem

The ecosystem is organized into five tiers. Tier 1 is the human operator — the only person in the loop. Tier 2 holds the manager bots that coordinate everything below them. Tier 3 is where the workers live: research, strategy building, and monitoring. Tier 4 contains the actual trading bots — both hand-built and pipeline-generated. Tier 5 is the foundation: databases, caches, and communication channels that every bot reads from and writes to.

The Playbook

Seven Phases, One Ecosystem

The AutoBots ecosystem was built from the ground up in seven sequential phases. Each phase unlocks the next capability — from database foundations and research infrastructure through autonomous strategy building, reporting, and eventually full orchestration.

Phase 1
Foundation
Done

Connected to the Blofin exchange, built the bot framework and process management layer, and migrated from SQLite to a production-grade SQL Server database. This is the infrastructure everything else runs on.

Blofin APIBot FrameworkPM2SQL Server
Why This Exists
Every bot needs a reliable foundation — a production database, a stable framework, and a process manager that keeps everything running. Without this, nothing else works.
2
Phase 2
Research Bot
Live

The strategy scout — scanning 14 sources across YouTube, GitHub, Reddit, and trading websites continuously. AI extracts trading rules, validates tickers against the exchange, and transcribes videos overnight using Whisper.

14 Active SourcesQwen2.5 14B494 IndicatorsWhisper GPU1,180 Videos Transcribed
Why This Exists
Manually finding trading strategies doesn’t scale. An automated scout that runs around the clock finds more ideas in a day than a human could find in a month.
3
Phase 3
Strategy Builder
Live

The autonomous pipeline — takes discovered strategies and puts them through 11 steps of testing. AI generates code, backtests against years of data, optimizes parameters, stress-tests through crashes, and auto-deploys survivors as paper trading bots.

38 Bots DeployedDynamic Golden NumbersGrouped RunnersWalk-Forward ValidationCrash Stress Testing
Why This Exists
Finding strategies is only half the problem — most of them don’t actually work. An autonomous pipeline that tests hundreds of ideas and only deploys the survivors eliminates guesswork.
4
Phase 4
Reporting Bot
Live

The messenger — consolidates all ecosystem activity into plain-English email reports sent four times: Morning Briefing at 9 AM, Evening Briefing at 8 PM, Weekly Trading Review on Sunday, and Strategy Comparison on Sunday. Five dedicated Telegram bots handle real-time alerts.

4 Email Reports5 Telegram BotsPlain EnglishLLM Error Translation
Why This Exists
With dozens of bots running simultaneously, checking each one individually isn’t practical. One consolidated report tells the full story without digging through dashboards.
5
Phase 5
Performance Monitor
Live

The judge — classifies weekly market conditions per ticker, compares strategies using normalized metrics, and generates monthly rankings with recommendations. Determines which strategy earns the one live trading slot per ticker.

Market ClassificationStrategy RankingsMonthly RollupsCorrelation AnalysisDaily Candle Updates
Why This Exists
Not every strategy works in every market. Tracking how each one performs across different conditions ensures the best strategy gets the live slot — not just the newest one.
6
Phase 6
News Bot
Next

The market watchdog — will monitor macro economic data releases like CPI, jobs reports, and Fed decisions, plus ticker-specific news like protocol upgrades, rebrandings, partnerships, and any developments that could move prices. Covers both the macro side and the buy-the-rumor-sell-the-news opportunities.

CPI/PPI/JobsFed DecisionsTicker-Specific NewsMarket IntelligenceTelegram Alerts
Why This Exists
Markets move on news before charts react. Knowing about a Fed decision or a major token event before it hits the price gives time to prepare instead of react.
7
Phase 7
Orchestrator
Planned

The coordinator — will manage scheduling across all bots, resolve resource conflicts, and centralize decision-making so the entire ecosystem operates as one synchronized system.

Bot CoordinationSchedulingConflict ResolutionFull Autonomy
Why This Exists
As the ecosystem grows, bots competing for GPU time, database connections, and scheduling windows creates conflicts. A central coordinator prevents bottlenecks and keeps everything synchronized.

System Security

Zero Vulnerabilities. Zero Secrets Leaked.

Every commit goes through automated security scanning before it can be merged. Bandit checks Python code for vulnerabilities, pip-audit flags dependency risks against CVE databases, and Gitleaks catches accidentally committed secrets. The ecosystem runs these checks continuously to keep the codebase clean.
